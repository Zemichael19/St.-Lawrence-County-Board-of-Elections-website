Absentee Voting

A voter may qualify to vote by absentee ballot if their duties, occupation, business or vacation requires them to be absent from their county of residence on election day or if they will be confined by illness, physical disability or for some other reason either on a temporary or permanent basis. Absentee ballot applications may be obtained by:

  • Writing the Board of Elections
  • Printing the appropriate form, and submitting that completed application to the BOE office
  • Calling 315-379-2202
  • E-mailing your request or print one of the forms that corresponds with your needs
    • Absentee Ballot

    Qualifications for the absentee ballot include:

  • Absent from St. Lawrence County on election day, or
  • Unable to appear at the polls due to illness or disability, or
  • Unable to appear at the polls due to duties related to primary care of one or more individuals who are ill or physically disabled
  • A patient in a Veteransâ€™ Administration Hospital, or
  • Detained in jail awaiting Grand Jury action or confined in prison after conviction for an offense other than a felony.
    • Permanently Disabled Absentee Ballot

    Please find the appropriate form. Qualifications for Permanently Disabled Absentee Ballot include:

  • Permanent Illness
  • Physical Disability
  • These voters will be put on a permanent list and receive a ballot for each election in which they are eligibile to vote.
    • Military Absentee Ballot

    Please find the appropriate form. Qualifications for Military Absentee Ballot include:

  • Actively in the Military, eligible spouse, or dependent
  • Activated National Guard member on State orders
  • Additional information can be found by visiting the Federal Voting Assistance Program website.
    • Special Federal Ballot

    Please find the appropriate form. Qualifications for Special Federal Ballot include:

  • For United States citizens living abroad whose last U.S. address was in St. Lawrence County
  • Applicants may only vote for Federal Offices, unless their application indicates an intent to return
    • Federal Write-in Absentee Ballot

    New York State requires you to register and request an absentee ballot by completing a Federal Post Card Application form. before filling out and submitting this backup form. This FPCA form is available above as well as by visiting the Federal Voting Assistance Program website

    Qualifications Federal Write-in Absentee Ballot include:

  • For use by Military voters, wherever they are posted
  • For use by Special Federal voters living outside the United States
  • If you already registered and requested an absentee ballot, send in the Voter Information page and the Official Backup Ballot.
  • Not for use by Regliar Absentee Voters or Permanent Absentee Voters.

    • If you do not receive your military or federal absentee ballot in enough time to meet New York State's deadlines, use this ballot as a backup. If you send in this ballot and then receive your official ballot later, you sholid fill out and send in that official ballot as well. Your election office will count only one ballot.
